Academics

Curriculum

St. Stephenes Bujwang'a Secondary School follows the Kenya Competency-Based Curriculum (CBC),
a modern curriculum designed to equip students with practical skills, competencies, and knowledge
essential for the 21st century. This curriculum replaces the previous 8-4-4 system and emphasizes holistic
education beyond rote learning, focusing on critical thinking, creativity, digital literacy, and life skills.

Subjects Offered

Junior Secondary (Grades 9-11): Core subjects include Mathematics, English, Kiswahili, Sciences (Biology, Chemistry, Physics),
Social Studies, Religious Education, and Creative Arts.

Exam Preparation

Students sit for a national exam at the end of Grade 9 that determines their learning pathway.

Senior Secondary (Grades 11-12): Students select specialized learning pathways according to their strengths and career interests

Curriculum Frameworks Followed

The CBC is structured around seven key competencies: Communication and Collaboration, Critical Thinking and Problem Solving, Creativity
and Imagination, Citizenship, Digital Literacy, Self-Efficacy, and Learning to Learn.

Our curriculum integrates theoretical knowledge with practical application and character development, aligning with Kenya’s Vision 2030.

Assessment & Schedules

Term Exams

At St. Stephenes Bujwang'a Secondary School, continuous assessment is an integral part of the learning process
under the Competency-Based Curriculum (CBC). Students undertake practical and written assessments throughout each term to monitor progress and competency acquisition.

Grading Policies

Our grading system focuses on proficiency in competencies rather than just numerical scores.

School Calendar Dates

The academic year is divided into three terms, each with a clear schedule for exams, holidays, and school events:

Term 1: January to April, with mid-term exams in March and term exams in April.

Term 2: May to August, with mid-term assessments in July and term exams in August.

Term 3: September to December, concluding with final exams in November and end-of-year activities in December.

Departments

St. Stephenes Bujwang'a Secondary School is proud to offer a diverse range of academic departments, each dedicated to delivering a well-rounded and competency-based education that meets
the standards of Kenya’s Competency-Based Curriculum (CBC). Our departments are staffed by experienced educators who are passionate about nurturing every student’s potential.

Science Department

Our Science Department provides comprehensive instruction in Physics, Chemistry, and Biology. Students engage in both theoretical learning and practical experiments, gaining critical
scientific skills and inquiry-based knowledge essential for STEM careers

Mathematics Department

The Mathematics Department focuses on building strong analytical and problem-solving capabilities. The curriculum includes algebra, geometry, calculus, and statistics to prepare for diverse academic and professional fields.

Languages Department

This department offers English and Kiswahili courses aimed at enhancing communication, comprehension, and literary skills. We emphasize reading, writing, and oral communication to foster language proficiency and cultural literacy.

Arts and Sports Department

This department nurtures creativity and physical well-being through creative arts, music, drama, and various sports activities. Students are encouraged to participate actively in extracurriculars for holistic growth

Admissions

How to Apply

Applications to St. Stephenes Bujwang'a Secondary School are accepted online and in person. Prospective students must fill out the official school admission form available on our website or at the school’s admission office. Early application is encouraged due to limited placements.

Eligibility

Admission is open to all students who have successfully completed the Kenya Certificate of Primary Education (KCPE) under the Competency-Based Curriculum (CBC) framework. Students must meet the minimum score criteria and demonstrate commitment to academic excellence and school values.

Open Days

We hold regular open days where prospective students and parents can visit the school, meet teachers, tour the facilities, and learn more about our programs and culture.

Required Documents

Applicants must submit the following documents with their application: Original and certified copy of KCPE or equivalent certificate,Birth certificate,Passport-size photographs, Recommendation letters,Completed application form

Student Life

Clubs & Societies

At St. Stephenes Bujwang'a Secondary School, student clubs and societies are a vital part of school life, offering opportunities for leadership, teamwork, and personal growth. Students may join a variety of clubs such as:

  • Debate Club
  • Science Club
  • Environmental Club
  • Drama & Music Society
  • Journalism Club
  • Christian Union
  • Wildlife Club
  • ICT Club

Participation in these clubs fosters self-confidence, organizational skills, and social responsibility. Wellbeing & Counseling

Student wellbeing is central to our values. The school provides comprehensive counseling services, including:

  • Academic guidance to help students set and achieve learning goals
  • Personal counseling to address emotional, behavioral, or social challenges
  • Peer mentoring programs and support groups

St. Stephenes Bujwang'a is committed to creating a caring and supportive environment where every student can thrive academically, socially, and emotionally.

Extracurricular Activities

St. Stephenes Bujwang'a Secondary School offers a wide range of extracurricular activities designed to enrich the student experience beyond the classroom. We believe in nurturing talents, creativity, and physical health alongside academic excellence.
